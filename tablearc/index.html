GreenKappa Labs flagship project

TableArc is a local-first AI tabletop engine.

It is being built as a structured, state-aware play system for tabletop RPG sessions: live narration at the surface, explicit adventure, character, rules, combat, map, continuity, and debug state underneath.

Collaboration direction Contact

What it is

Not just an AI narrator. A runtime for playable scenes.

TableArc explores how an AI-assisted Game Master can remain imaginative while being grounded by structured state: who is present, what the scene contains, what the player knows, what hidden facts remain locked, what rules apply, what changed, and why.

The project began as an AI-DM kernel and evolved into a broader engine architecture for solo play, DM support, adventure generation, character repair, rules lookup, recap/state tools, tactical maps, and runtime debugging.

Core systems

Designed around structure, flexibility, and transparency.

Live Play

Natural-language play supported by scene state, player intent, consequences, continuity, and responsive narration.

Adventure Frameworks

Adventure generation aimed at scenes, nodes, clues, clocks, NPC agendas, encounters, and open-ended approaches.

Character Authority

Character import, repair, active-PC state, resources, inventory, party context, and rules-aware action handling.

Maps & Combat

Tactical scene context, movement, targeting, enemy state, HP, action economy, spatial truth, and aftermath tracking.

Rules & Recap

Rules lookup, state recap, scene memory, continuity tools, and debug-friendly explanations of what happened.

Local Model Support

Built toward local-first workflows with provider flexibility, including local language models through Ollama.

Design philosophy

Authored structure. Flexible play. Visible state.

TableArc is strongest where freeform language and structured game logic meet. The long-term design goal is to let players and creators keep the expressive freedom of tabletop play while making the underlying game state coherent, inspectable, and recoverable.

Collaboration direction

Scene packets for authored adventures.

One promising direction is using TableArc’s runtime thinking to support existing human-authored adventures, rather than replacing them.

Branch-safe flexible resolution

A publisher-approved adventure section could be converted into a structured scene packet: visible facts, hidden facts, available branches, checks, enemies, items, map anchors, spoiler rules, and flexible resolution options.

An assistant could then help players try creative approaches that are not explicitly listed while still preserving the authored module: interpret the action, check rules and context, avoid spoilers, map the result to a valid branch or bounded consequence, and add transition flavor into the next section.

  • Spoiler-safe context
  • Branch-compatible play
  • Rules-aware action checks
  • Map and encounter metadata
  • Authored content remains primary
  • AI adds responsiveness, not replacement
